Ad breaks are kind of lovely

My friend C addressed the movie night crew about her Netflix subscription: "there are ads but that just gives us time to talk" (or something like that). It made me think about how I used to perceive ad breaks. Running to the bathroom to make it back in time, sneaking into the kitchen to grab a snack, unpacking something from the show with my friends in the living room. As I reminisced, I realized how much I had missed this, and how weird it is that I might be apart of the last generation to experience it.

It also got me thinking about personal agency. With ad breaks, I only gave big screens my partial attention. I couldn't spend an entire evening watching a show because the channel would eventually stop playing its episodes. If a movie was going to end at 7pm and I missed 15 minutes of it, the movie would still end at 7pm. I didn't experience the mild guilt of sitting still for hours in front of my TV, and I'm confident my screen time was lower, too. Strangely, when I had less control over the when and what of the media I consumed, it felt like I had more freedom.

(Thank you C for inspiring this)
